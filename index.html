Enactus DDUC strives to reach new milestones to engender an environment that is more potent and thus aims to create an impact and uplift communities. With our business models, we help the community in optimal utilization of their skills and resources.

PROJECT PEHEL

Pehel, an initiative by Enactus KMV, strives to empower underprivileged women by providing them with vocational training and additional income opportunities. Our mission is to unlock the potential of the true architects of our society - women. Since the inception of Project Pehel, we have made a meaningful impact in the lives of numerous underprivileged women who have been eager to work but lacked the opportunities to do so. Through Pehel, we offer a range of stylish and eco-friendly tote bags that are not only affordable but also help eliminate single-use plastic. In addition to this, we are committed to providing impoverished women with the necessary skills to create more opportunities for themselves and their families in the future. Join us in our mission to uplift and support these inspiring women.

PROJECT SAHAAYTA

Project Sahaayta is an initiative by Enactus KMV in a bid to spread and save the dying art of pottery and get our artisans the recognition they deserve. By cutting out the middlemen and establishing a direct market link using both offline and online platforms, the project seeks to empower the Potter's community. Our project is currently working with the underprivileged potters in West Delhi. We observed that the community is in grave danger as a result of the widespread use of affordable plastic alternatives today. We have conducted a few field visits as a part of the project Sahaayta, where the team interacted with the local Potter's community to learn about their art form and how they run their day-to-day operations. During our interactions with these potters. we discovered that the main issue that the community faces is heavy competition from the less expensive plastic products. Team Sahaayta is working to promote terracotta products and raise public awareness in order to help the community The field visits help us understand the plight of the potter's community, and the goal is to interact with them in order to solve these issues and help the local craftsmen. Until now, we have skilled/employed 10+ and educated more than 30+ potters. Raised the monthly income of 10+ families from 15,000 to 25,000. Persuaded 10 restaurants to switch to earthenware. Sold 10.000+ Kulhads to Cafes and tea shops Sold a variety of more than 15 terracotta products and thus eliminating 180kg of plastic.

Our Stalls

To promote our initiatives and goods, we have taken the initiative to set up stalls in several colleges. We've run more than ten booths in several universities, including shivaji, vivekanda, jmc, etc., and we've had success selling our goods and raising awareness of our programmes. We have been using the greatest marketing techniques and obtaining positive feedback from our clients. These booths have been entertaining while while providing experience. The use of stalls has greatly benefited enactus. Our first stall was in March at Bharati College in their Holi fest Phagun'22. All the stalls were a great success in increasing the awareness about dying arts of India and the vulnerable communities. The main highlight of these stalls is when people asked us about the artists and enthusiastically stepped up to help them. Our motto for the stalls was to spread stories and smiles.
